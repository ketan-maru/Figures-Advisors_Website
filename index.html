Why We Built This

For decades, the best financial expertise — experienced CFOs, seasoned controllers, and strategic tax advisors — was reserved for companies with millions in revenue. Small business owners, solopreneurs, and growing teams were left to navigate the most important financial decisions of their journey largely on their own.

The early years of building a business are the most defining. How you structure your company, manage cash flow, and make strategic decisions at this stage shapes everything that follows — yet most founders are forced to do it without the financial guidance that larger companies take for granted.

We built Figures to change that. Our mission is to make world-class financial leadership accessible to every business building something meaningful.

Common questions

Answers for owners comparing CFO advisory, bookkeeping, and tax support.

What does Figures Advisors do?

Figures Advisors gives small businesses a CFO-led finance team: CFO advisory, monthly bookkeeping, financial statements, accounts payable and receivable support, and real-time tax planning.

How much does it cost?

Figures Foundations is a flat $999 per month. Businesses with higher volume, multi-entity structures, or more complex finance needs can ask about the Operator plan.

Who is this best for?

It is designed for solopreneurs, consultants, e-commerce brands, product businesses, professional services firms, launch-stage companies, and owner-operated businesses that need more than basic bookkeeping.

Does this replace my accountant or CPA?

It depends on what you need. If you're engaging us for CFO advisory only, we work alongside your existing accountant or CPA — not instead of them. Your current relationship stays intact, and we add a strategic finance layer on top. If you take our full bundle — CFO advisory, bookkeeping, finance operations, and tax — then yes, we become your complete finance team and your existing accountant or CPA would be replaced. Most clients find the bundle is the better fit, but we're happy to scope either way.
